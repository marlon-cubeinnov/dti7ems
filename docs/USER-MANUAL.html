1. System Overview

The DTI Events Management System (DTI EMS) is a web-based platform developed for the Department of Trade and Industry, Region VII — Central Visayas. It provides end-to-end management of training events for Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) — from proposal creation to post-training effectiveness evaluation.

Key Features

  • Event Proposal Workflow — Structured 7 QMS-step process for creating, reviewing, and approving training proposals
  • Participant Registration — Online registration with RSVP confirmation and Training Needs Assessment (TNA)
  • QR Code Attendance — Digital check-in via QR code scanning for real-time attendance tracking
  • Client Satisfaction Feedback (CSF) — ARTA-prescribed survey format with SQD dimensions and official reporting
  • Certificate Issuance — Digital certificates with unique verification codes
  • Post-Activity Reporting — PAR (FM-CT-6) and Training Effectiveness Monitoring (FM-CT-5)
  • Enterprise Directory — Searchable public directory of registered MSMEs
  • Audit Trail — Comprehensive logging of all administrative actions

7. Training Needs Assessment (TNA)

Some events require participants to complete a Training Needs Assessment before confirming their RSVP. This helps organizers tailor the training content.

1
Navigate to the TNA form

Go to My Events in the sidebar. Find the event and click "Complete TNA →".

2
Answer the questions

Rate yourself on a scale of 1–10 for each question across three areas:

  • Knowledge (3 questions) — Business concepts, best practices, DTI programs
  • Skills (3 questions) — Applying skills, using tools, managing operations
  • Motivation (2 questions) — Drive to improve, willingness to adopt innovations
3
Click "Submit TNA & Confirm RSVP"

Your participation status changes to RSVP_CONFIRMED and your personal QR code becomes available.

There are no right or wrong answers. Your responses are used for training design only.

8. Your QR Code

Once your RSVP is confirmed, you receive a unique QR code for attendance tracking.

1
Access your QR code

Go to My Events → find the event → click "📱 My QR Code".

2
Present at the event

Show the QR code on your screen to the facilitator for scanning at the venue. The QR code is your permanent personal check-in code for that event.

Your QR code is only available when your status is RSVP_CONFIRMED or ATTENDED.

9. Client Satisfaction Feedback (CSF) Survey

After an event is completed, participants are asked to fill out the CSF survey — the official ARTA-prescribed feedback form.

1
Access the CSF survey

Go to My Events → find the completed event → click "📋 Complete Feedback Survey →".

2
Section 1: Citizen's Charter (CC)

Answer three questions about the Citizen's Charter:

  • CC1 — Awareness: Your awareness of the Citizen's Charter
  • CC2 — Visibility: How easy it was to see the CC in the office/venue
  • CC3 — Usefulness: How much the CC helped you
3
Section 2: Service Quality Dimensions (SQD)

Rate each dimension on a 1–5 scale (1 = Strongly Disagree, 5 = Strongly Agree):

CodeDimensionQuestion Focus
SQD0Overall SatisfactionI am satisfied with the service I availed
SQD1ResponsivenessStaff responded to my needs promptly
SQD2ReliabilityService was delivered within expected timeframe
SQD3Access & FacilitiesOffices/facilities were accessible and comfortable
SQD4CommunicationInformation was clear and easy to understand
SQD5CostsFees were reasonable (if applicable)
SQD6IntegrityStaff performed duties professionally
SQD7AssuranceService instilled trust and confidence
SQD8OutcomeService/training met its intended purpose
4
Section 3: Speaker Ratings

Rate each speaker on a 1–5 star scale based on their presentation effectiveness.

5
Section 4: Open-ended Feedback

Provide written feedback in the text areas for: Highlights/What you liked, Suggestions for improvement, Comments/Suggestions, and Reasons for low ratings (if any).

6
Submit the survey

Click "Submit Survey". Your status will show "Survey Submitted ✓" on the My Events page.

10. Impact Survey — FM-CT-5 (Training Monitoring and Evaluation Form)

The Impact Survey follows DTI's official FM-CT-5 Training Monitoring and Evaluation Form (Version 1, effective February 1, 2024). It is sent to participants 6 months after the conduct of training to measure long-term effectiveness and impact on business operations.

1
Email Notification

Six months after the training, you will receive an email notification informing you that the FM-CT-5 Impact Survey is ready to be completed. The email contains a link to your My Events page.

2
Access the Impact Survey

Go to My Events → find the event → click "📊 Complete Impact Survey →" (shown beside the "Survey Submitted ✓" badge). The survey form opens with the official DTI header including Doc Code FM-CT-5.

3
Star Ratings (1–5)

Rate these areas: Knowledge Application, Skill Improvement, Business Impact, Revenue Change, Employee Growth.

4
FM-CT-5 Training Effectiveness Evaluation

This section mirrors the official DTI form. Complete the following:

  • Applied Learnings: Indicate Yes or No whether you applied the training learnings
  • Benefit Indicators (a–p): Check all that apply — Increased sales (%), Increased profit (%), Cost reduction (%), Accessed new markets, Improved productivity, Improved manpower welfare, Standardized operations, Started/improved bookkeeping, Improved management systems, Set up new business, Expanded business, Enhanced capacity, Adopted technology, Innovation, No customer complaints, Others (specify)
  • Additional Assistance Needed: Product development, Loan advisory, Others
  • Future Training: Specify what training programs you'd like to attend
  • Training Effectiveness: Yes/No with reason if "No"
  • Respondent Info: Your designation/position and company/business name
5
Submit

Click "Submit Impact Assessment". Your response is recorded, and the "📊 Complete Impact Survey →" button changes to "Impact Survey Submitted ✓" in purple on your My Events page.

15. Proposal Approval Workflow

Proposals follow a structured approval chain mapped to the DTI QMS process.

DRAFT
→
SUBMITTED
→
UNDER REVIEW
→
APPROVED

Step-by-Step Approval Process

1
Technical Staff submits the proposal

On the proposal detail page, click "Submit for Review". The status changes from DRAFT to SUBMITTED.

2
Division Chief reviews

The Division Chief sees the proposal in their "Proposals Queue". After reviewing, click "Mark Under Review". Status changes to UNDER_REVIEW.

3
Regional/Provincial Director decides

The Director sees the proposal in "For Approval". Options:

  • "Approve" → Status: APPROVED
  • "Reject" → Status: REJECTED (requires a rejection note)
If a proposal is rejected, the Technical Staff can edit the proposal based on the rejection note and resubmit it.

16. Activating Events

After a proposal is approved, the Technical Staff must activate the event to make it visible to the public.

1
Assign a Facilitator (optional but recommended)

On the proposal page, use the staff search field to find and select a Facilitator (EVENT_ORGANIZER role). Click "Assign Facilitator".

2
Click "Activate Event & Start Pre-Activity"

A green panel shows Step 3 of the QMS process. Click the activation button to:

  • Change the event status from DRAFT to PUBLISHED
  • Auto-generate the DTI Training Monitoring Checklist (FM-CT-7)
  • Make the event visible on the public events listing
Once an event is activated, the proposal becomes locked and can no longer be edited.

33. Frequently Asked Questions

General

Q: Which web browsers are supported?

DTI EMS works best on modern browsers: Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, and Safari. Mobile browsers are also supported with responsive layout.

Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?

Click "Forgot password?" on the login page. Enter your email address to receive a reset link. Follow the link to set a new password.

Q: I didn't receive the verification email.

Check your spam/junk folder. If still not found, contact the System Administrator to manually verify your email address.

Participants

Q: How do I get my certificate?

After attending the event, the organizer will issue your certificate. Check My Certificates in the sidebar. You can print or download it as PDF.

Q: I can't see the "Register" button on an event.

Possible reasons: (1) You're not logged in — click "Log in" first. (2) Registration is not yet open. (3) The event is full. (4) You've already registered for this event.

Q: What is TNA?

Training Needs Assessment is a short questionnaire that helps organizers understand your current knowledge and skills. Some events require it before confirming your RSVP. There are no wrong answers.

Q: When should I complete the Impact Survey?

The Impact Survey (FM-CT-5) is sent approximately 6 months after the training to measure long-term effectiveness. You will be notified when it becomes available.

Staff

Q: Why can't I create a proposal?

Only users with the Technical Staff (PROGRAM_MANAGER) role or admin roles can create proposals. Facilitators (EVENT_ORGANIZER) manage assigned events but cannot create proposals.

Q: The QR scanner isn't working. What can I do?

Ensure the event status is ONGOING. Try switching to a different camera if available. Use the "Manual Check-in" fallback to enter the Participation ID directly.

Q: How do I assign a facilitator to an event?

On the proposal page (after approval), use the staff search field to find and select the Facilitator. Click "Assign Facilitator". This should be done before activating the event.

Q: What happens when I activate an event?

Activation publishes the event (makes it visible to participants), auto-generates the FM-CT-7 checklist with standard DTI items, and locks the proposal from further editing.
