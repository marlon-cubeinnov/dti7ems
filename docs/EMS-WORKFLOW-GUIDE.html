2. Registration & Account Setup

2.1 Who should register?

Anyone who will attend, organize, or manage DTI Region 7 training and enterprise development events. Choose the category that best describes you during sign-up:

CategoryWho registers hereNotes
Individual / CitizenGeneral public, solo entrepreneurs, freelancersBasic profile, no enterprise affiliation required
Government EmployeeLGU staff, national government personnelSets clientType = GOVERNMENT
NGO / OrganizationCivil society, cooperatives, associationsCreates an organization profile automatically
Business / MSMERegistered businesses, MSMEsCreates an enterprise profile; DTI verifies

2.2 Step-by-step registration

  1. Go to the EMS portal and click Register

    Navigate to the public portal URL and click the Register button on the top navigation bar.

  2. Choose your registration category

    Select the tab that matches your type: Individual/Citizen, Government Employee, NGO/Organization, or Business/MSME.

  3. Fill in your personal details

    Provide your first name, last name, email address, mobile number, and password. MSME/NGO registrants also enter business information (name, sector, stage).

    📧 A verification email is sent to the address you provide. You must verify it before you can fully access your account.
  4. Submit and verify your email

    Click the link in the verification email to activate your account.

  5. Complete your participant profile

    Log in and go to My Profile. Fill in optional demographic details (sex, age bracket, employment category) — these are used in ARTA/CSC reports.

⚠ Duplicate company check: If your company name is already in the system, the registration form will show a 409 Conflict notice. Contact the System Administrator to link your existing account instead of creating a duplicate.

4. Participant Workflow

Participant
  1. Browse upcoming events

    From the Dashboard or Events page, find events relevant to your business or interest. Use the search filters (sector, location, date) to narrow results.

  2. Register for an event

    Click the event card, review the details, and click Register for this Event. If you are a Business/MSME user, you may be prompted to confirm your business stage before proceeding.

    Business stage (Pre-Startup → Startup → Growth → Expansion → Mature) determines the programs you are eligible for. Update it if your stage has changed.
  3. Complete the TNA Survey (pre-training)

    After registering, complete the Training Needs Assessment survey. This captures your current knowledge level before the training — it is used to measure learning progress after the event.

    The TNA should be completed before the event date. Submit it as early as possible so organizers can tailor the session to participants' needs.
  4. Attend the event and get your QR code scanned

    On event day, present your QR attendance code (found in My Events → Event Details) at the registration desk. Your attendance is recorded per session.

  5. Complete the CSF Survey (post-training)

    After the event, submit the Client Satisfaction Form. This evaluates the trainer, venue, materials, and overall experience. You can access it from My Events — look for the Pending badge.

    💡 Your organizer can share a QR code that takes you directly to your CSF form if you cannot find it.
  6. Download your certificate

    Once the organizer submits the post-activity report, your certificate of participation becomes available. Go to My Certificates or click the certificate banner on your Dashboard to download.

5. Organizer / Program Manager Workflow

Organizer

5.1 Creating a New Event Proposal

  1. Go to New Proposal

    From the sidebar, click New Proposal and fill in the event details: title, description, type, target sector, dates, venue, and capacity.

  2. Attach the checklist

    Add your pre-event checklist items (logistics, materials, speakers). These will be tracked on the event dashboard.

  3. Submit for approval

    Click Submit for Review. The proposal moves to the Program Manager / Division Chief for endorsement.

  4. Address revision requests (if any)

    If feedback is received, update the proposal and re-submit. Track comments in the proposal timeline.

5.2 Managing an Approved Event

  1. Open the event in My Events

    Once approved, your event appears under My Events. Click it to open the event dashboard.

  2. Monitor registrations

    View the Participant List tab. You can see registration status, TNA completion, and CSF status for each participant.

  3. Send CSF QR codes for pending surveys

    In the Participant List, participants with a Pending CSF status show a QR icon. Click it to generate and download/share a QR code that links directly to that participant's CSF form.

  4. Record attendance on event day

    Use the QR Attendance scanner in the event dashboard to scan participants' codes and record their attendance per session/day.

  5. Review CSF results

    After the event, go to CSF Results to see all responses. Low-rated submissions (any SQD score ≤ 2) are highlighted in red with an alert banner at the top. Use this to identify areas for improvement.

  6. Submit the Post-Activity Report

    Complete the Post-Activity Report (narrative + actual expenses). Once submitted, the system generates certificates for all qualified participants.

6. Admin Workflow

Admin

6.1 User Management

  1. Access Users from the Admin sidebar

    Search by name or email. Use the role and status filters to narrow the list.

  2. Review and activate accounts

    New participant accounts registered via the portal are Active by default. You can suspend, ban, or change a user's role via the Actions menu.

  3. Verify email addresses manually

    If a user did not receive the verification email, use Verify Email from the user detail actions.

6.2 Proposal Approval

  1. Open All Events → Proposals

    Proposals in Pending Review status are listed first. Click a proposal to open the full details.

  2. Review proposal details

    Check objectives, budget, target sector, proposed dates, and checklist.

  3. Approve, request revision, or reject

    Use the action buttons at the top of the proposal page. Approved proposals are published and become discoverable by participants.

7. Enterprise Management (Admin)

Admin

Navigate to Enterprises in the Admin sidebar to manage all registered MSME and organization profiles.

7.1 Viewing and Filtering Enterprises

  • Use the search box to find enterprises by business name or TIN.
  • Filter by Stage (Pre-Startup, Startup, Growth, Expansion, Mature).
  • Filter by Verified status to find unverified enterprises awaiting review.

7.2 Verifying an Enterprise

Click Verify in the Actions column. This marks the enterprise as officially recognized by DTI. Verified status is shown with a green checkmark in the list and on the participant's profile.

7.3 Listing / Unlisting an Enterprise

Click List to make the enterprise publicly visible in the EMS Business Directory. Click Unlist to hide it. Only publicly listed enterprises appear in directory searches.

7.4 Editing an Enterprise

  1. Click the pencil (✏️) icon in the Actions column

    The Edit Enterprise modal opens with all current details pre-filled.

  2. Update the required fields

    You can change: Business Name, Trade Name, Registration No., TIN, Industry Sector, Business Stage, Employee Count, Annual Revenue, Region, Province, City/Municipality, and toggle Verified / Publicly Listed.

  3. Click Save Changes

    Changes take effect immediately. An audit log entry is created automatically.

7.5 Deleting an Enterprise

⚠ Destructive action: Deleting an enterprise permanently removes its profile and all associated membership records. This cannot be undone. Only delete duplicates or test entries.
  1. Click the trash (🗑) icon in the Actions column

    A confirmation dialog appears showing the enterprise name.

  2. Confirm deletion

    Click Delete Enterprise to proceed. The action is logged in the Audit Log under the ENTERPRISE_DELETED event.

7.6 Linking a Participant to an Existing Enterprise

If a participant registered and created a duplicate enterprise profile, do the following:

  1. Delete the duplicate enterprise via the Enterprises page.
  2. Open the participant's profile in the Users page and note their user ID.
  3. In the correct enterprise, contact the enterprise owner to add the participant via Enterprise Members → Add Member by Email.

8. Surveys & Evaluation

8.1 Training Needs Assessment (TNA)

The TNA is a pre-training baseline survey completed by participants when they register for an event. It measures their existing knowledge and identifies skill gaps before the training begins.

Results from the TNA are used by organizers to confirm topic priorities and by the program team to measure the improvement achieved after training (pre- vs. post-event comparison).

8.2 Client Satisfaction Form (CSF)

The CSF is a post-training evaluation form completed by participants after the event ends. It rates eight Service Quality Dimensions (SQD 1–8) on a 1–5 scale.

DimensionWhat it measures
SQD 1Timeliness of service
SQD 2Waiting time
SQD 3Responsiveness of staff
SQD 4Knowledge & competence
SQD 5Helpfulness / courtesy
SQD 6Quality of service delivery
SQD 7Overall satisfaction
SQD 8Would you recommend this service?
Responses with any SQD score of 1 or 2 (Very Dissatisfied / Dissatisfied) are automatically flagged in the CSF Results page with a red alert banner. Organizers should review these individually and address concerns in the post-activity report.

9. Certificates

Certificates of participation are generated automatically once the organizer submits the post-activity report for a completed event.

For Participants

  1. Log in and go to Dashboard. A blue certificate banner appears when new certificates are available.
  2. Click View Certificates → or navigate to My Certificates from the sidebar.
  3. Click Download to get a PDF copy of your certificate.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Participant must have attended at least one recorded session (QR-scanned attendance).
  • Completion of the CSF survey is recommended but not required by default.
  • The post-activity report must be submitted and accepted by the admin.
